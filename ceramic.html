5 different types of ceramic Coating

Ceramic Coating

Ceramic coatings come in different grades, typically based on the durability, level of protection, and chemical composition. These grades are usually categorized by the amount of SiO2 (Silicon Dioxide) content, longevity, and additional features they offer. Here's a breakdown of the common grades of ceramic coatings:

1.Entry-Level ceramic Coating
SiO2 Content: 30%-50% Durability: 1-2 years Key Features:

  • Affordable: Great for car owners who want a ceramic coating without a long term commitment.
  • Good Shine: Offers a noticeable improvement in glass and shine compared to wax or sealants.
  • Basic Protection: Provides basic UV protection, chemical resistance, and hydrophobic properties (water beading).
  • Ease of Use: Often available as spray-on or wipe-on products, making them easier to apply.
Best For: car enthusiasts or owners looking for short-term protection or those who frequently reapply coatings.

2.Mid-Grade Ceramic Coating
SiO2 Content: 50%-70% Durability: 2-5 years Key Features:
  • Enhanced Durability: Lasts longer with stronger protection against environmental contaminants, road salts, and minor abrasions.
  • Better Hydraphobic properties: Increased water-repelling and dirt resistance, making the car easier to clean.
  • UV & Chemical resistance: Offers strong UV protection, preventing fading, and higher resistance to bird droppings, tree sap, and acidic contaminants.
  • Self-cleaning Effect: Some mid-grade coatings provide a self-cleaning ability, where dirt and water slide off easily. Best For: Car Owners seeking longer-term protection with moderate effort in maintenance. It'sideal for daily drivers who want more robust protection.


3.Professional-Grade Ceramic Coating
SiO2 content: 70%-95% Durability: 5-7 years (or more with proper care) Key Features:
  • Maximum Protection: Offers the highest level of protection against scratches, chemical, UV damage and oxidation.
  • High Gloss & Depth: Provides a deep, mirror-like glass that enhances the paint's natural color.
  • Extreme Hydrophobicity: Water and contaminants bead and roll off the surface with little effort.
  • Scratch Resistance: Provides improved protection against minor abrasions, though it won't prevent major scratches.
  • Requires Professional Application: These coatings typically require professional installation, ensuring proper bonding and longevity.
Best For: Luxury or performance car owners who want the ultimate protection and long-lasting shine. This is the go-to option for enthusiasts or collectors looking to preserve their vehicle's appearance for the long term.

4.Graphene Ceramic Coating (Advanced Grade)
SiO2 + Graphene Content: 85%-95% Durability: 5-10 years Key Features:
  • Advanced Technology: Incorporates graphene, which provides higher thermal conductivity, anti-static properties, and better chemical resistance than traditional ceramic coatings,
  • Superb Hydrophobic Properties: Strong water-repelling and anti-water spot capabilities due to the addition of graphene.
  • Heat DissipationL Graphene helps dissipate heat more effectively, reducing the risk of water spots.
  • Increased Scratch & Chemical Resistance: Offers a step up in scratch and chemical resistance compared to standard ceramic coatings.
Best For: Car owners looking for cutting-edge protection with the highest performance in terms of hydrophobic properties, durability, and ease of maintenance.

5.Hybrid Ceramic Coating
SiO2 + Other polymers Varies (20%-60%) Durability: 6 months to 1 year Key Features:
  • Blended Technology: Combines ceramic protection with other polymers or waxes for added flexibility and shine.
  • Quick Application: Typically easier and faster to apply than traditional ceramic coatings, available as spray-on or wipe-on products.
  • Enhanced Shine: Adds a glossy, wet-look finish to the vehicle.
  • Lower Durability: While providing good protection, it doesn't last as long as pure ceramic coatings.
Best For: Those who want ceramic-like protection without a full proffesional-grade treatment. Ideal for DIY enthusiasts or car owners who don't mind more frequent applications.

Each grade offers its own benefits based on your needs and budget, making it important to match the coating to the expectations and use case.
  • Entry-level coatings provide affordable, short-term protection.
  • Mid-grade coatings offer a balance between protection, durability and cost.
  • Professional-grade coatings are for those who want long-lasting, high-end protection with professional installation.
  • Fraphene coatings represent the latest in advanced protection technology.
  • Hybrid ceramic coatings are for easy, temporary use with ceramic properties.
